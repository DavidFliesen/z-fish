## Fat Accumulation Targeting and Localized Adipose Signaling

FATLAS is an AI-assisted biomedical research initiative exploring how the human body determines where excess energy is stored and why some individuals are more prone to unhealthy abdominal fat accumulation than others.

Using specialized AI research agents focused on hormones, genetics, gut microbiome activity, inflammation, aging, and adipose tissue biology, FATLAS investigates the signaling pathways that influence fat storage, fat distribution, and metabolic health.

## Mission

To identify the biological mechanisms that regulate fat storage location and uncover safe, science-based intervention targets that may improve metabolic health and reduce obesity-related disease.

## Research Areas

- Hormone Signaling
- Genetics & Epigenetics
- Gut Microbiome
- Inflammation & Immune Function
- Aging & Metabolic Change
- Adipose Tissue Biology
- Systems-Level Integration

## Current Status

FATLAS-001 established the initial multi-agent research structure and produced the first coordinated synthesis of biological pathways associated with visceral abdominal fat accumulation.

Future rounds will expand the research to compare visceral fat, abdominal subcutaneous fat, and lower-body subcutaneous fat, with emphasis on why the body stores excess energy in different locations.
